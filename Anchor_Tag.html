Google

Google is a multinational technology company best known for its search engine, which helps users find information on the internet. Founded in 1998, it has grown to offer a wide range of services like Gmail, Google Maps, YouTube, Google Drive, Android, and more. It plays a major role in digital advertising, cloud computing, and artificial intelligence, and is a part of the parent company Alphabet Inc.

YouTube

YouTube is a free online video-sharing platform where users can watch, upload, share, and comment on videos. Launched in 2005 and owned by Google, it is one of the most visited websites globally. YouTube hosts a wide range of content, including music videos, tutorials, vlogs, educational content, gaming, and live streams. It allows creators to earn money through ads, memberships, and sponsorships, making it a major platform for entertainment, learning, and digital careers.

Google Map

Google Maps (GMaps) is a web-based mapping and navigation service developed by Google. It provides real-time directions, location search, traffic updates, satellite imagery, street views, and route planning for driving, walking, biking, and public transport. Users can explore businesses, landmarks, restaurants, and local services, as well as share their location or reviews. It is widely used worldwide for both daily commutes and travel planning.

Gmail

Gmail is a free email service developed by Google. It allows users to send, receive, and organize emails efficiently with features like spam filtering, labels, conversation threads, and powerful search. Launched in 2004, Gmail is known for its large storage space, integration with other Google services (like Google Drive, Calendar, and Meet), and its availability on both web and mobile platforms. It is widely used for personal, educational, and professional communication around the world.
